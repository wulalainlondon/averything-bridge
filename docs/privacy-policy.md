# Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2026-06-01

Averything is a mobile companion app for controlling AI runtimes through a desktop bridge that you install and run on your own computer.

## Data We Collect

The app developer does not operate a hosted backend for Averything and does not collect app messages, files, voice input, local network addresses, or AI session content by default.

The app stores configuration and session state on your device so it can reconnect to your bridge.

## Data Sent To Your Desktop Bridge

When you connect Averything to your desktop bridge, the app sends the data needed to use the bridge, such as chat messages, selected files, voice input text, bridge settings, push notification tokens if enabled, and connection metadata.

That bridge runs on infrastructure controlled by you, usually your own computer on the same Wi-Fi network. The app developer does not receive this bridge traffic unless you configure the bridge to send it to a service controlled by the developer.

## Voice Input

If you use voice input, the app requests microphone and speech recognition permissions. Voice input is only used when you start speech input. Platform speech recognition services may process audio according to the operating system provider's policies.

## Local Network

Averything uses local network access to discover and connect to your desktop bridge on the same Wi-Fi network.

## Notifications

If you enable push notifications, the app may register a push notification token and send it to your desktop bridge so the bridge can notify you when an AI task finishes. Notifications are optional.

## Data Sharing

The app developer does not sell your data and does not share your personal data with advertisers.

Data may be processed by platform services you choose to use, such as Apple or Google push notifications, platform speech recognition, or any AI runtime and desktop bridge configuration you operate.

## Retention And Deletion

Data stored by the app remains on your device until you delete it in the app, clear app storage, or uninstall the app. Data stored by your desktop bridge is controlled by your bridge installation.

## Security

Averything connects to your configured desktop bridge. Use trusted networks and configure bridge authentication where appropriate.
